In principle, in order for this expansion to be valid, the term $(AdA - BdB)/\omega^2$ should be small, i.e. $Z_c \rightarrow 1$. However, in our fit, we found that $Z_c$ is close to 2 for LaCuO, and even larger than 2 for SrCuO. The first order approximation breaks down in those cases.

To estimate the error brought by this approximation: We know that this approximation is simply $\sqrt{1 + \epsilon} \approx 1 + \frac{\epsilon}{2} = Z_c$. If $Z_c = 2$, then $\epsilon = 2$. If we use the exact formula, the result without approximation should be $\sqrt{1+2} \approx 1.7$. Therefore, the relative error is $(2-1.7)/1.7 \approx 18\%$.

Therefore, we probably need to use the exact formula for $Z_c$ for those cases. From here on, we call the method without the approximation (1) precise-self-consistent-loop and (2) precise-direct-Zc_calculation.

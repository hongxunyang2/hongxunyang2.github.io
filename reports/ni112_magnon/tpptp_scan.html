Fit to Dispersion with tpptp Scanning

I performed the loop extraction fitting with scanning over different values of $t^{\prime\prime}/t^{\prime}$ (denoted as tpptp in the code). This allows us to find the optimal value of tpptp that minimizes the chi-square.

I used two methods: 1. direct-zc-calculation: $Z_c$ is directly calculated during the fit 2. precise-zc-calculation: $Z_c$ is calculated using the exact expression

In the fitting process:

  • $U$ is the only free parameter for the fit.

  • $t^{\prime\prime}/t^{\prime}$ is now being looped over. denoted as tpptp in the code.

  • $\frac{t}{U}$ and $\frac{t^\prime}{t}$ are being looped over. denoted as tU and tpt in the code.

  • For each combination of tpptp, tU, and tpt, we fit the data with the only free parameter $U$. With the resulting $U$, we calculate the $\chi^2$. In effect, $\chi^2(\frac{t}{U}, \frac{t^\prime}{t}, \frac{t^{\prime\prime}}{t^{\prime}})$ is a function of $\frac{t}{U}$, $\frac{t^\prime}{t}$, and $\frac{t^{\prime\prime}}{t^{\prime}}$.

  • At the end of the fit, we plot out the $\chi^2$ landscape for each tpptp value, and pick the best parameters set.

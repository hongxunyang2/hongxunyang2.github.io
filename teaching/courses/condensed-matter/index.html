Tutorial Session

Interactive problem-solving sessions

During tutorial sessions, students are invited to present their solutions to the exercises in front of the class. As the teaching assistant, I provide comments on their solutions, clarify common misconceptions, and offer further insights on the topics covered in each exercise.

This format encourages active participation and helps students develop their ability to communicate physics concepts clearly—a valuable skill for both exams and future careers.

Exercise Sheets

A curated mix of classic and new problems

The exercises draw from an existing pool from last year's course, supplemented with new problems I designed myself. This hybrid approach was necessary for two reasons:

First, this year's course arrangement differs from last year, so some adjustments were needed. Second, I wanted to create exercises that help students better bridge the conceptual gaps between different topics, while also providing them with extra insights that go beyond standard textbook problems.

Examination

Oral exam format

The course concludes with an oral examination. I will be part of the examiner team alongside the course instructor.

  • 1 Topic Presentation (10 min) — The student presents a randomly selected topic from the course material, demonstrating their understanding and ability to explain the concepts.
  • 2 Exercise Walk-through — An exercise is picked from the exercise sheets, and the student walks us through their approach and solution.
